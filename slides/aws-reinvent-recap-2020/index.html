    • Lambda Billing Granularity​

    What does this mean?​

  • If you are outside of the free tier of Lambda, your bill automatic becomes cheaper
  • The percent change on your function will vary based upon how much Lambda was previously rounding up

    • Request Charges

    Request charges don’t change

    Old

    60 million invocations
    * $0.20 per 1M requests
    = $12

    New

    60 million invocations
    * $0.20 per 1M requests
    = $12

    Request Charges

    Request charges have changed

    Old

    60 million invocations
    * 100ms
    * 1G memory
    * $0.0000166667 for every GB-second
    = $100​

    New

    60 million invocations
    * 28ms
    * 1G memory
    * $0.0000166667 for every GB-second
    = $28​

    Lambda is already cheap​​

    Why bring this up? Before reengineering consider the ROI on your developers’ time

  • Each business is different
  • Developer $23.60/hr – average for Knoxville based on payscale.com
  • 2 Days to gain 25% efficiency
  • Developer cost = 2 days * 8 hours * $23.60/hour = $377.60 to make the function 25% faster
